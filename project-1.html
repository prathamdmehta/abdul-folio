Research Paper

Project Overview

This study focuses on e-bikes, particularly the 'Electric bike' category under Swedish standards, which adhere to specific motor power and speed limitations. Although the European Union provides general guidelines, these standards vary across countries within Europe, necessitating a detailed analysis centered on Sweden. The research evaluates e-bikes' features such as power, comfort, cost, and reliability, considering various technical and geographical conditions as well as public awareness and interest. A comparative analysis with ordinary bicycles and other vehicles highlights e-bikes' advantages in cost-effectiveness, power efficiency, leisure services, accessibility, and environmental impact, especially for short-distance and inner-city travel. The study also delves into the technical aspects of e-bike components, examining their operation, effectiveness in power consumption and energy dispatching, and overall performance quality.

To gain a better understanding of the market, a short survey was conducted to assess public awareness and perceptions regarding e-bikes. The survey results, along with other analyzed factors, helped predict the potential development and popularity of e-bikes in Sweden. The findings suggest that what qualifies as an Electric bike in the U.S. may differ from Sweden due to varying national standards. According to European classification, an Electric bike must have a motor capacity of up to 750 W and automatically stop the motor when the speed exceeds 25 km/h.
